Dataset Overview

The dataset used in this analysis comprises health and lifestyle data for a sample of individuals. Before delving into the statistical analysis, let's provide an overview of the dataset:

  • Total Number of Rows: 375
  • Total Number of Columns: 13

Dataset Columns:

  • Person ID: An identifier for each individual.
  • Gender: The gender of the person (Male/Female).
  • Age: The age of the person in years.
  • Occupation: The occupation or profession of the person.
  • Sleep Duration (hours): The number of hours the person sleeps per day.
  • Quality of Sleep (scale: 1-10): A subjective rating of the quality of sleep, ranging from 1 to 10.
  • Physical Activity Level (minutes/day): The number of minutes the person engages in physical activity daily.
  • Stress Level (scale: 1-10): A subjective rating of the stress level experienced by the person, ranging from 1 to 10.
  • BMI Category: The BMI category of the person (e.g., Underweight, Normal, Overweight).
  • Blood Pressure (systolic/diastolic): The blood pressure measurement of the person, indicated as systolic pressure over diastolic pressure.
  • Heart Rate (bpm): The resting heart rate of the person in beats per minute.
  • Daily Steps: The number of steps the person takes per day.
  • Sleep Disorder: The presence or absence of a sleep disorder in the person (None, Insomnia, Sleep Apnea).

Details about Sleep Disorder Column:

  • None: The individual does not exhibit any specific sleep disorder.
  • Insomnia: The individual experiences difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, leading to inadequate or poor-quality sleep.
  • Sleep Apnea: The individual suffers from pauses in breathing during sleep, resulting in disrupted sleep patterns and potential health risks.

Hypothesis Testing

Hypothesis 1: Difference in Sleep Duration Between Genders

We are interested in investigating whether there is a significant difference in sleep duration between genders. To test this hypothesis, we will perform a hypothesis test, such as a t-test for independent samples, comparing the sleep duration of males and females in our dataset.

Null Hypothesis (H0): There is no significant difference in sleep duration between genders.
Alternative Hypothesis (H1): There is a significant difference in sleep duration between genders.

We will conduct the appropriate statistical test and report the results, including the test statistic, p-value, and effect size (if applicable). If the p-value is less than a predetermined significance level (e.g., 0.05), we will reject the null hypothesis in favor of the alternative hypothesis.

In[]:
# Define the two groups based on gender
males = df[df['Gender'] == 'Male']['Sleep Duration']
females = df[df['Gender'] == 'Female']['Sleep Duration']

# Perform the t-test for independent samples
t_statistic, p_value = stats.ttest_ind(males, females)

# Set the significance level
alpha = 0.05

# Print the results
print("Hypothesis 1: Difference in Sleep Duration Between Genders")
print("Null Hypothesis (H0): There is no significant difference in sleep duration between genders.")
print("Alternative Hypothesis (H1): There is a significant difference in sleep duration between genders.")
print()
print("Test Statistic:", t_statistic)
print("p-value:", p_value)
print()

# Check if the null hypothesis should be rejected
if p_value < alpha:
    print("Result: Reject the null hypothesis.")
    print("Conclusion: There is a significant difference in sleep duration between genders.")
else:
    print("Result: Fail to reject the null hypothesis.")
    print("Conclusion: There is no significant difference in sleep duration between genders.")

Hypothesis 2: Relationship Between Physical Activity Level and Sleep Quality

We want to explore the potential relationship between physical activity level and sleep quality. Specifically, we aim to determine whether individuals with higher physical activity levels tend to have better sleep quality scores. To test this hypothesis, we will use correlation analysis.

Null Hypothesis (H0): There is no significant correlation between physical activity level and sleep quality.
Alternative Hypothesis (H1): There is a significant correlation between physical activity level and sleep quality.

We will calculate the correlation coefficient (e.g., Pearson's correlation coefficient) between physical activity level and sleep quality and assess its statistical significance. Additionally, we will provide a scatter plot to visualize the relationship between these variables.

The results of these hypothesis tests will help us draw conclusions about the differences in sleep duration between genders and the potential relationship between physical activity level and sleep quality in our dataset.
